COLLEGE 101

Introduction

College life is one of the biggest transitions we go through in our lives. It is like stepping into a world of new experiences, learning, and the biggest of all, independence. College is a big step for college students, and it's a time when they navigate the world of academia while also growing in their personal lives. The halls of college education are not only filled with academic discourse and lectures, but also with a symphony of college life. It is more than just a place of higher education; it is a place where a variety of experiences come together to shape young people's identities. For many students, it is the first time when they navigate the intricate balance of academic responsibilities, social engagements, and personal development without the familiar environment of home. As they attend lectures, study late into the night, and take part in the hustle and bustle of campus life, they embark on a journey of intellectual and personal growth.

  • Here is what I did:

    To better understand the change from students' perspectives, I asked a bunch of college students how their life changed after they started their college education. I asked them about the challenges they faced, differences they felt, and how they dealt with them. As we explore the stories of our friends, we are trying to know how they've grown, from making lifelong friends to understanding themselves and the world better. In the following slides, there are the transcripts of my discussions with those students:

  • Noah Olantuji

    Me

    Tell me How has your life changed after coming to college?

    Noah

    I have definitely noticed a lot more independence and responsibility. Since coming to college, I have been able to make my own schedule and my own decisions about all my academics and my day to day life; and academically there are a lot of different subjects and courses that I can pursue so that that made me curious about what I actually have to do and there are also a lot of different people here, with a lot of different kinds of backgrounds so I am able to view a lot of different experiences and being away from the familiar environment of my home, it allows personal growth and self-discovery so I can learn a lot about myself and what I actually enjoy doing.

    Me

    Right, and what was the biggest difference you felt between your high school and college?

    Noah

    The biggest difference between the college and my high school is probably the size. Where I went to high school, my graduating class was very small, it was a little less then forty people. So, the switch from that to a really large school like Texas Tech, it’s just that I can’t know everybody like I did when I was in high school.

    Noah Olantuji is a Computer Science major who just started his college journey. He expresses a sense of positive transformation and growth as he discusses his experiences in college. He enjoys the freedom to shape his own academic path and appreciate the diversity of people and experiences in his new environment. Overall, he is optimistic and enthusiastic about the opportunities and learning experiences that the college has brought.

  • Faiyaz Mostafa

    Me

Stating from various cultures like eating habits to staying in dorms, everything changed the way I used to live back in my home. I had to do laundry and studies were there obviously, but I had to do a lot of things that didn’t do. So time management was the most challenging thing for me.

    Faiyaz Mostafa is a freshman who also happens to be Computer Science majors. He conveys a mix of challenges and adaptability in his college experience. Being an international student, he has experienced alot of changes and cultural shocks. Despite the considerable changes, he maintains a positive outlook, viewing these adjustments as preparation for the future. He seems to navigate the complexities of college life with a willingness to embrace change, tempered by the recognition of the difficulties associated with increased independence and responsibilities.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the narratives of these people provide a multifaceted insight into the transformative journey of college life. Across these diverse experiences, we can observe a common thread emerging—the profound impact of newfound independence and responsibility.

Noah embraces the freedom to shape his academic path and values the diversity of experiences that college offers. Faiyaz acknowledges the challenges of cultural shock and time management while maintaining a positive outlook on change as preparation for the future. Barzillai grapples with the significant shift to independence, particularly in financial matters, recognizing the sacrifices made for personal growth. Alex underscores the lessons in discipline, time management, and financial resilience gained through the college experience. Abid appreciates the new environment and new people, and wants to understand their mindsets.

Collectively, these stories highlight the numerous ways in which college serves as a catalyst for intellectual and personal development. The shift from the familiar boundaries of high school to the expansive landscape of college education teaches adaptation, resilience, and a redefinition of self. Challenges such as cultural adjustments, time management, and financial independence become motivates for growth, fostering a deeper understanding of one's capabilities and passions.

Through the lens of these people, we witness the metamorphosis from wide-eyed freshmen to individuals who, despite the trials, emerge with a newfound sense of purpose, resilience, and an eagerness to confront the ever-evolving landscape of life beyond the college gates.
